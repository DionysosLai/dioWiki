## 5.2 Projective Camera Models

One of the fundamental issues in 3D computer graphics is the *3D viewing problem:* how to project a 3D scene onto a 2D image for display. Most of the classic approaches can be expressed by a projective transformation matrix. Therefore, we will introduce a projection matrix camera class, ProjectiveCamera, and then define two camera models based on it. The first implements an orthographic projection, and the other implements a perspective projection—two classic and widely used projections.

> 3D 计算机图形学的基本问题之一是 *3D 观察问题*：如何将 3D 场景投影到 2D 图像上进行显示。大多数经典方法可以用投影变换矩阵来表达。因此，我们将引入一个投影矩阵相机类 ProjectiveCamera，然后基于它定义两个相机模型。第一个实现正交投影，另一个实现透视投影——两种经典且广泛使用的投影。

The orthographic and perspective projections both require the specification of two planes perpendicular to the viewing direction: the *near* and *far* planes. When rasterization is used for rendering, objects that are not between those two planes are culled. In a ray tracer, the projection matrices are used purely to determine rays leaving the camera and these concerns do not apply.

> 正交投影和透视投影都需要指定两个垂直于观察方向的平面：*近平面*和*远平面*。当使用光栅化进行渲染时，不在这两个平面之间的物体被剔除。在光线追踪器中，投影矩阵纯粹用于确定从相机发出的光线，这些问题不适用。

Three more coordinate systems are useful for defining and discussing projective cameras:

> 另外三个坐标系对定义和讨论投影相机很有用：

- *Screen space:* Screen space is defined on the film plane. Points at the near plane are mapped to a depth value of 0 and points at the far plane are mapped to 1.
- *Normalized device coordinate (NDC) space:* This is the coordinate system for the actual image being rendered. In x and y, this space ranges from (0, 0) to (1, 1), with (0, 0) being the upper-left corner of the image.
- *Raster space:* This is almost the same as NDC space, except the x and y coordinates range from (0, 0) to the resolution of the image in x and y pixels.

> - *屏幕空间*：屏幕空间定义在胶片平面上。近平面上的点映射到深度值 0，远平面上的点映射到 1。
> - *归一化设备坐标 (NDC) 空间*：这是正在渲染的实际图像的坐标系。在 x 和 y 方向上，该空间范围从 (0, 0) 到 (1, 1)，其中 (0, 0) 是图像的左上角。
> - *光栅空间*：与 NDC 空间几乎相同，只是 x 和 y 坐标范围从 (0, 0) 到图像在 x 和 y 方向上的像素分辨率。

### 5.2.1 Orthographic Camera

The orthographic camera is based on the orthographic projection transformation. The orthographic transformation takes a rectangular region of the scene and projects it onto the front face of the box that defines the region. It does not give the effect of *foreshortening*—objects becoming smaller on the image plane as they get farther away—but it does leave parallel lines parallel, and it preserves relative distance between objects.

> 正交相机基于正交投影变换。正交变换取场景的一个矩形区域，将其投影到定义该区域的盒子的前面。它不产生*透视缩短*效果（物体在图像平面上随着距离增远而变小），但它保持平行线平行，并保持物体之间的相对距离。

The orthographic viewing transformation leaves x and y coordinates unchanged but maps z values at the near plane to 0 and values at the far plane to 1. To do this, the scene is first translated along the z axis so that the near plane is aligned with z = 0. Then, the scene is scaled in z so that the far plane maps to z = 1.

> 正交观察变换保持 x 和 y 坐标不变，但将近平面处的 z 值映射到 0，远平面处的 z 值映射到 1。为此，首先沿 z 轴平移场景使近平面与 z = 0 对齐，然后在 z 方向上缩放场景使远平面映射到 z = 1。

Thanks to the simplicity of the orthographic projection, it is easy to directly compute the differential rays in the x and y directions. The directions of the differential rays will be the same as the main ray (as they are for all rays generated by an orthographic camera), and the difference in origins will be the same for all rays. Therefore, the constructor precomputes how much the ray origins shift in camera space coordinates due to a single pixel shift in the x and y directions on the film plane.

> 由于正交投影的简单性，很容易直接计算 x 和 y 方向上的微分光线。微分光线的方向与主光线相同（正交相机生成的所有光线都如此），且原点差异对所有光线都相同。因此，构造函数预计算胶片平面上 x 和 y 方向单像素偏移在相机空间坐标中导致的光线原点偏移量。

We can now go through the code that takes a sample point in raster space and turns it into a camera ray. First, the raster space sample position is transformed into a point in camera space, giving a point located on the near plane, which is the origin of the camera ray. Because the camera space viewing direction points down the z axis, the camera space ray direction is (0, 0, 1).

> 我们现在可以来看将光栅空间中的采样点转换为相机光线的代码。首先，光栅空间采样位置被变换为相机空间中的点，给出位于近平面上的点，即相机光线的原点。由于相机空间的观察方向沿 z 轴，相机空间光线方向为 (0, 0, 1)。

If the lens aperture is not a pinhole, the ray's origin and direction are modified so that defocus blur is simulated. Finally, the ray is transformed into rendering space before being returned.

> 如果镜头光圈不是针孔，光线的原点和方向会被修改以模拟散焦模糊。最后，光线在返回之前被变换到渲染空间。

### 5.2.2 Perspective Camera

The perspective projection is similar to the orthographic projection in that it projects a volume of space onto a 2D film plane. However, it includes the effect of foreshortening: objects that are far away are projected to be smaller than objects of the same size that are closer. Unlike the orthographic projection, the perspective projection does not preserve distances or angles, and parallel lines no longer remain parallel. The perspective projection is a reasonably close match to how an eye or camera lens generates images of the 3D world.

> 透视投影与正交投影类似，都是将空间体积投影到 2D 胶片平面上。然而，它包含透视缩短效果：远处的物体被投影为比同样大小但距离更近的物体更小。与正交投影不同，透视投影不保持距离或角度，平行线也不再保持平行。透视投影与眼睛或相机镜头生成 3D 世界图像的方式相当接近。

The perspective projection describes perspective viewing of the scene. The Perspective() function computes this transformation; it takes a field-of-view angle in fov and the distances to a near plane and a far plane. The transformation is most easily understood in two steps:

> 透视投影描述场景的透视观察。Perspective() 函数计算此变换；它接受 fov 中的视野角和到近平面与远平面的距离。该变换最容易通过两个步骤理解：

1. Points in camera space are projected onto the viewing plane. The projected x' and y' coordinates can be computed by dividing x and y by the point's z coordinate value. The projected depth is remapped so that values at the near plane are 0 and values at the far plane are 1. This can be encoded in a 4×4 matrix.

> 1. 相机空间中的点被投影到观察平面上。投影后的 x' 和 y' 坐标可以通过将 x 和 y 除以点的 z 坐标值来计算。投影深度被重新映射，使近平面处的值为 0，远平面处的值为 1。这可以编码在 4×4 矩阵中。

2. The angular field of view (fov) specified by the user is accounted for by scaling the values on the projection plane so that points inside the field of view project to coordinates between [-1, 1] on the view plane. Scaling by the reciprocal of tan(fov/2) maps the field of view to the range [-1, 1].

> 2. 用户指定的角视场 (fov) 通过缩放投影平面上的值来考虑，使视场内的点投影到视图平面上 [-1, 1] 之间的坐标。按 tan(fov/2) 的倒数缩放将视场映射到 [-1, 1] 范围。

With the perspective projection, camera space rays all originate from the origin, (0, 0, 0). A ray's direction is given by the vector from the origin to the point on the near plane, pCamera, that corresponds to the provided CameraSample's pFilm location.

> 对于透视投影，相机空间光线全部从原点 (0, 0, 0) 发出。光线方向由从原点到近平面上对应于提供的 CameraSample 的 pFilm 位置的点 pCamera 的向量给出。

The cosine of the maximum angle of the perspective camera's field of view will occasionally be useful. In particular, points outside the field of view can be quickly culled via a dot product with the viewing direction and comparison to this value.

> 透视相机视场最大角度的余弦偶尔会有用。特别是，视场外的点可以通过与观察方向的点积并与此值比较来快速剔除。

### 5.2.3 The Thin Lens Model and Depth of Field

An ideal pinhole camera that only allows rays passing through a single point to reach the film is not physically realizable; while it is possible to make cameras with extremely small apertures that approach this behavior, small apertures allow relatively little light to reach the film sensor. With a small aperture, long exposure times are required to capture enough photons to accurately capture the image, which in turn can lead to blur from objects in the scene moving while the camera shutter is open.

> 只允许通过单一点的光线到达胶片的理想针孔相机在物理上是不可实现的；虽然可以制造具有极小光圈的相机来接近这种行为，但小光圈允许到达胶片传感器的光线相对较少。使用小光圈时，需要较长的曝光时间来捕获足够的光子以准确捕捉图像，而这反过来可能导致场景中物体在相机快门打开时移动而产生的模糊。

Real cameras have lens systems that focus light through a finite-sized aperture onto the film plane. Camera designers (and photographers using cameras with adjustable apertures) face a trade-off: the larger the aperture, the more light reaches the film and the shorter the exposures that are needed. However, lenses can only focus on a single plane (the *focal plane*), and the farther objects in the scene are from this plane, the blurrier they are. The larger the aperture, the more pronounced this effect is.

> 真实的相机具有透镜系统，通过有限大小的光圈将光聚焦到胶片平面上。相机设计师（和使用可调光圈相机的摄影师）面临权衡：光圈越大，到达胶片的光越多，所需的曝光时间越短。然而，透镜只能聚焦在单一平面（*焦平面*）上，场景中的物体离该平面越远就越模糊。光圈越大，这种效果就越明显。

For the simple camera models, we can apply the *thin lens approximation* to model the effect of finite apertures. Under the thin lens approximation, incident rays that are parallel to the optical axis and pass through the lens focus at a point behind the lens called the *focal point*. The distance the focal point is behind the lens, f, is the lens's *focal length*. If the film plane is placed at a distance equal to the focal length behind the lens, then objects infinitely far away will be in focus.

> 对于简单的相机模型，我们可以应用*薄透镜近似*来建模有限光圈的效果。在薄透镜近似下，平行于光轴并通过透镜的入射光线聚焦在透镜后方称为*焦点*的一个点。焦点在透镜后方的距离 f 是透镜的*焦距*。如果胶片平面放置在透镜后方等于焦距的距离处，则无穷远处的物体将对焦。

For points in the scene at a depth z from a thin lens with focal length f, the *Gaussian lens equation* relates the distances from the object to the lens and from the lens to the image of the point:

> 对于场景中距薄透镜深度为 z 的点（透镜焦距为 f），*高斯透镜方程*关联了从物体到透镜和从透镜到点的像之间的距离：

1/z' - 1/z = 1/f (Equation 5.1)

> 1/z' - 1/z = 1/f（公式 5.1）

A point that does not lie on the plane of focus is imaged to a disk on the film plane, rather than to a single point. The boundary of this disk is called the *circle of confusion*. The size of the circle of confusion is affected by the diameter of the aperture, the focal distance, and the distance between the object and the lens. The range of depths that appear in focus are termed the *depth of field*.

> 不位于对焦平面上的点在胶片平面上成像为一个圆盘，而非单个点。该圆盘的边界称为*弥散圆*。弥散圆的大小受光圈直径、焦距和物体与透镜之间距离的影响。看起来对焦清晰的深度范围称为*景深*。

The diameter of the lens is often expressed as the lens's *f-number*, which expresses diameter as a fraction of focal length, n = f/d.

> 透镜直径通常表示为透镜的 *f 值*（光圈数），它将直径表示为焦距的分数，n = f/d。

Modeling a thin lens in a ray tracer is remarkably straightforward: all that is necessary is to choose a point on the lens and find the appropriate ray that starts on the lens at that point such that objects in the plane of focus are in focus on the film. Therefore, projective cameras take two extra parameters for depth of field: one sets the size of the lens aperture, and the other sets the focal distance.

> 在光线追踪器中建模薄透镜非常直接：只需在透镜上选择一个点，并找到从该点出发的适当光线，使得对焦平面上的物体在胶片上对焦。因此，投影相机为景深效果接受两个额外参数：一个设置镜头光圈大小，另一个设置焦距。

It is generally necessary to trace many rays for each image pixel in order to adequately sample the lens for smooth defocus blur.

> 通常需要为每个图像像素追踪许多条光线，以充分采样透镜以获得平滑的散焦模糊。

The SampleUniformDiskConcentric() function takes a sample position in [0, 1)² and maps it to a 2D unit disk centered at the origin. To turn this into a point on the lens, these coordinates are scaled by the lens radius.

> SampleUniformDiskConcentric() 函数接受 [0, 1)² 中的样本位置并将其映射到以原点为中心的 2D 单位圆盘。将这些坐标按透镜半径缩放即可得到透镜上的点。

The ray's origin is this point on the lens. We know that *all* rays from the given image sample through the lens must converge at the same point on the plane of focus. Finding the appropriate point of convergence is a matter of intersecting the unperturbed ray from the pinhole model with the plane of focus and then setting the new ray's direction to be the vector from the point on the lens to the intersection point.

> 光线的原点是透镜上的这个点。我们知道，从给定图像样本通过透镜的*所有*光线必须汇聚在对焦平面上的同一点。找到适当的汇聚点只需将针孔模型中未扰动的光线与对焦平面相交，然后将新光线的方向设为从透镜上的点到交点的向量。

To compute ray differentials with the thin lens, the same approach is applied to rays offset one pixel in the x and y directions on the film plane.

> 为了计算薄透镜的光线微分，将相同的方法应用于在胶片平面上 x 和 y 方向各偏移一个像素的光线。
