## 3.5 Normals

A *surface normal* (or just *normal*) is a vector that is perpendicular to a surface at a particular position. It can be defined as the cross product of any two nonparallel vectors that are tangent to the surface at a point. Although normals are superficially similar to vectors, it is important to distinguish between the two of them: because normals are defined in terms of their relationship to a particular surface, they behave differently than vectors in some situations, particularly when applying transformations. (That difference is discussed in Section [3.10](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/Applying_Transformations.html#sec:applying-transforms).)

> *表面法线*（或简称*法线*）是在某一位置处垂直于表面的向量。它可以定义为在某点处与表面相切的任意两个不平行向量的叉积。虽然法线表面上与向量类似，但区分两者很重要：因为法线是根据它们与特定表面的关系来定义的，在某些情况下它们的行为与向量不同，特别是在应用变换时。（这一差异在第 3.10 节中讨论。）

The implementations of [Normal3](#Normal3) s and [Vector3](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/Vectors.html#Vector3) s are very similar. Like vectors, normals are represented by three components x, y, and z; they can be added and subtracted to compute new normals; and they can be scaled and normalized. However, a normal cannot be added to a point, and one cannot take the cross product of two normals. Note that, in an unfortunate turn of terminology, normals are *not* necessarily normalized.

> Normal3 和 Vector3 的实现非常相似。与向量一样，法线由三个分量 x、y 和 z 表示；它们可以相加和相减来计算新的法线；也可以进行缩放和归一化。但是，法线不能与点相加，也不能对两个法线取叉积。请注意，在一个不幸的术语巧合中，法线（normal）不一定是*归一化的*（normalized）。

In addition to the usual constructors (not included here), Normal3 allows conversion from [Vector3](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/Vectors.html#Vector3) values given an explicit typecast, similarly to the other [Tuple2](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/n-Tuple_Base_Classes.html#Tuple2) - and [Tuple3](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/n-Tuple_Base_Classes.html#Tuple3) -based classes.

> 除了常规构造函数（此处不包含）外，Normal3 允许通过显式类型转换从 Vector3 值进行转换，与其他基于 Tuple2 和 Tuple3 的类类似。

One new operation to implement comes from the fact that it is often necessary to flip a surface normal so it lies in the same hemisphere as a given vector—for example, the surface normal that lies in the same hemisphere as a ray leaving a surface is frequently needed. The FaceForward() utility function encapsulates this small computation. (pbrt also provides variants of this function for the other three combinations of [Vector3](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/Vectors.html#Vector3) s and [Normal3](#Normal3) s as parameters.) Be careful when using the other instances, though: when using the version that takes two [Vector3](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Geometry_and_Transformations/Vectors.html#Vector3) s, for example, ensure that the first parameter is the one that should be returned (possibly flipped) and the second is the one to test against. Reversing the two parameters will give unexpected results.

> 需要实现的一个新操作源于这样一个事实：经常需要翻转表面法线使其与给定向量位于同一半球——例如，经常需要与离开表面的光线位于同一半球的表面法线。FaceForward() 实用函数封装了这一小计算。（pbrt 还为 Vector3 和 Normal3 的其他三种参数组合提供了该函数的变体。）不过使用其他版本时要小心：例如当使用接受两个 Vector3 的版本时，确保第一个参数是应该返回（可能翻转）的那个，第二个是用来测试的。颠倒两个参数会产生意想不到的结果。
