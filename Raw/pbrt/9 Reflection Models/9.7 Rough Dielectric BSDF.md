## 9.7 Rough Dielectric BSDF

We will now extend the microfacet approach from Section [9.6](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Roughness_Using_Microfacet_Theory.html#sec:microfacet) to the case of rough dielectrics. This involves two notable changes: since dielectrics are characterized by both reflection and transmission, the model must be aware of these two separate components. In the case of transmission, Snell's law will furthermore replace the law of reflection in the computation that determines the incident direction.

> 我们现在将第 9.6 节中的微面方法扩展到粗糙电介质的情况。这涉及两个显著的变化：由于电介质同时具有反射和透射特性，模型必须知道这两个独立的分量。在透射情况下，斯涅耳定律将在确定入射方向的计算中替代反射定律。

Figure [9.32](#fig:dragon-ts) shows the dragon rendered with the Torrance–Sparrow model and both reflection and transmission.

> 图 9.32 展示了使用 Torrance-Sparrow 模型同时包含反射和透射来渲染的龙模型。

Figure 9.32: Dragon models rendered with the Torrance–Sparrow microfacet model featuring both reflection (a) and transmission (b). *(Model courtesy of Christian Schüller.)*

> 图 9.32：使用 Torrance-Sparrow 微面模型渲染的龙模型，同时包含反射 (a) 和透射 (b)。*（模型由 Christian Schüller 提供。）*

As before, we will begin by characterizing the probability density of generated samples. The implied BSDF then directly follows from this density and the sequence of events encapsulated by a scattering interaction: visible normal sampling, reflection or refraction, and attenuation by the Fresnel and masking terms.

> 与之前一样，我们将首先表征生成样本的概率密度。隐含的 BSDF 然后直接由该密度和散射交互所封装的事件序列得出：可见法线采样、反射或折射、以及菲涅耳和遮蔽项的衰减。
