## 9.3 Specular Reflection and Transmission

Following the discussion of diffuse surfaces with their perfectly uniform reflectance, we now turn to the opposite extreme: specular materials that only reflect light into a discrete set of directions. Following a review of the physical principles underlying such materials in this section, we will introduce concrete BxDF implementations in Sections [9.4](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Conductor_BRDF.html#sec:smooth-conductors) and [9.5](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Dielectric_BSDF.html#sec:smooth-dielectrics).

> 在讨论了具有完全均匀反射率的漫反射表面之后，我们现在转向相反的极端：仅将光反射到离散方向集合中的镜面材料。在本节回顾这些材料的物理原理之后，我们将在第 9.4 节和第 9.5 节中介绍具体的 BxDF 实现。

Our initial focus is on perfect specular surfaces. However, many real-world materials are fairly rough at a microscopic scale, and this can have a profound influence on their reflection behavior. Sections [9.6](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Roughness_Using_Microfacet_Theory.html#sec:microfacet) and [9.7](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Rough_Dielectric_BSDF.html#sec:rough-dielectric) will generalize our understanding of the perfect specular case to such rough surface microstructures.

> 我们最初关注的是完美镜面。然而，许多真实世界的材料在微观尺度上相当粗糙，这会对其反射行为产生深远影响。第 9.6 节和第 9.7 节将把我们对完美镜面情况的理解推广到这种粗糙表面微结构。

### 9.3.1 Physical Principles

For the most part, this book is concerned with *geometric optics*, which describes the scattering and transport of radiance along rays. This is an approximation of the wave nature of light, albeit an excellent one: visible light waves occur at scales that are negligible (m) compared to the size of objects rendered in pbrt ( millimeters to meters), and hence wave-like phenomena normally do not manifest in rendered images.

> 本书大部分内容关注*几何光学*，它描述辐射亮度沿光线的散射和传输。这是对光的波动性质的近似，尽管是一个非常好的近似：可见光波发生在与 pbrt 渲染的物体尺寸（毫米到米）相比可以忽略不计的尺度（微米级）上，因此波动现象通常不会在渲染图像中表现出来。

Yet, to understand and model what happens when light strikes a surface, it is helpful to briefly turn toward this deeper understanding of light in terms of waves. Using wave-optical results within an overall geometric simulation is often possible and has become a common design pattern in computer graphics.

> 然而，为了理解和建模光照射到表面时发生的情况，简要转向从波的角度对光的更深层理解是有帮助的。在整体几何模拟中使用波动光学结果通常是可能的，并已成为计算机图形学中的常见设计模式。

The theory of electromagnetism describes light as an oscillation of the *electric and magnetic fields*. What does this mean? These terms refer to *vector fields*, which are convenient mathematical abstractions that assign a 3D vector to every point in space. These vectors describe the force that a small charged particle would feel due to such a light wave passing around it. For our purposes, only the electric field is interesting, and the charged particle that will be influenced by this force is an *electron* surrounding the nucleus of an atom.

> 电磁理论将光描述为*电场和磁场*的振荡。这意味着什么？这些术语指的是*向量场*，它们是方便的数学抽象，为空间中的每一点分配一个三维向量。这些向量描述了光波经过时小带电粒子所感受到的力。对于我们的目的，只有电场是有趣的，受此力影响的带电粒子是围绕原子核的*电子*。

When a beam of light arrives at a surface, it stimulates the electrons of the atoms comprising the material, causing them to begin to oscillate rapidly. These moving electric charges induce secondary oscillations in the electric field, whose superposition is then subject to constructive and destructive interference. This constitutes the main mechanism in which atoms reflect light, though the specifics of this process can vary significantly based on the type of atom and the way in which it is bound to other atoms. The electromagnetic theory of light distinguishes the following three major classes of behaviors.

> 当一束光到达表面时，它会刺激构成材料的原子的电子，使它们开始快速振荡。这些运动的电荷在电场中产生二次振荡，其叠加受到相长和相消干涉的影响。这构成了原子反射光的主要机制，尽管该过程的细节可能因原子类型及其与其他原子的结合方式而显著不同。光的电磁理论区分了以下三大类行为。

The large class of *dielectrics* includes any substance (whether gaseous, liquid, or solid) that acts as an electric insulator, including glass, water, mineral oil, and air. In such materials, the oscillating electrons are firmly bound to their atoms. Note that a liquid like water can be made electrically conductive by adding ions (e.g., table salt), but that is irrelevant in this classification of purely atomic properties.

> *电介质*这一大类包括任何充当电绝缘体的物质（无论是气态、液态还是固态），包括玻璃、水、矿物油和空气。在这些材料中，振荡的电子被牢固地束缚在原子上。请注意，像水这样的液体可以通过添加离子（如食盐）而变得导电，但这在纯粹原子属性的分类中是无关的。

The second class of electric *conductors* includes metals and metal alloys, but also semi-metals like graphite. Some of the electrons can freely move within their atomic lattice; hence an oscillation induced by an incident electromagnetic wave can move electrons over larger distances. At the same time, migration through the lattice dissipates some of the incident energy in the form of heat, causing rapid absorption of the light wave as it travels deeper into the material. Total absorption typically occurs within the top 0.1 m of the material; hence only extremely thin metal films are capable of transmitting appreciable amounts of light. We ignore this effect in pbrt and treat metallic surfaces as opaque.

> 第二类电*导体*包括金属和合金，还有像石墨这样的半金属。一些电子可以在原子晶格中自由移动；因此入射电磁波引起的振荡可以使电子移动更大的距离。同时，通过晶格的迁移以热的形式耗散了部分入射能量，导致光波在深入材料时被快速吸收。完全吸收通常发生在材料顶部 0.1 微米内；因此只有极薄的金属薄膜才能透射可观的光量。我们在 pbrt 中忽略这种效应，将金属表面视为不透明的。

A third class of *semiconductors*, such as silicon or germanium, exhibits properties of both dielectrics and conductors. For example, silicon appears metallic in the visible spectrum. At the same time, its transparency in the infrared range makes it an excellent material for optical elements in IR cameras. We do not explicitly consider semiconductors in pbrt, though adding a suitable BxDF to handle them would be relatively easy.

> 第三类*半导体*，如硅或锗，同时表现出电介质和导体的特性。例如，硅在可见光谱中呈现金属外观。同时，它在红外范围内的透明性使其成为红外相机光学元件的优良材料。我们在 pbrt 中没有明确考虑半导体，尽管添加合适的 BxDF 来处理它们相对容易。

### 9.3.2 The Index of Refraction

When an incident light wave stimulates an electron, the oscillation induces its own electromagnetic oscillation. The oscillation of this re-emitted light incurs a small delay compared to the original wave. The compound effect of many such delays within a solid material causes the light wave to travel at a slower velocity compared to the original speed of light.

> 当入射光波刺激电子时，振荡会引发其自身的电磁振荡。这种再发射光的振荡与原始波相比会产生一个小的延迟。在固体材料内部许多这样的延迟的复合效应使得光波以比原始光速更慢的速度传播。

The speed reduction is commonly summarized using the *index of refraction* (IOR). For example, a material with an IOR of 2 propagates light at half the speed of light. For common materials, the value is in the range 1.0–2.5 and furthermore varies with the wavelength of light. We will use the Greek letter, pronounced "eta," to denote this quantity.

> 速度降低通常用*折射率*（IOR）来概括。例如，折射率为 2 的材料使光以光速的一半传播。对于常见材料，该值在 1.0–2.5 的范围内，并且随光的波长而变化。我们将使用希腊字母 η（读作"eta"）来表示这个量。

Light waves undergo significant reflection when they encounter boundaries with a sudden change in the IOR value. For example, an air–diamond interface with a comparably high IOR difference of 2.42 will appear more reflective than an air–glass surface with a difference around 1.5. In this sense, the IOR provides the main mathematical explanation of why we perceive objects around us: it is because their IOR differs from the surrounding medium (e.g., air). The specific value of controls the appearance of surfaces; hence a good estimate of this value is important for physically based rendering.

> 当光波遇到折射率值突然变化的边界时，会发生显著的反射。例如，空气-钻石界面的折射率差为 2.42，比折射率差约为 1.5 的空气-玻璃表面看起来更具反射性。从这个意义上说，折射率提供了我们为什么能感知周围物体的主要数学解释：这是因为它们的折射率与周围介质（如空气）不同。折射率的具体值控制着表面的外观；因此对该值的良好估计对于基于物理的渲染非常重要。

We first review *in which direction(s)* light travels following an interaction, which is described by the law of specular reflection and Snell's law. ### 9.3.4 Snell's Law

### 9.3.5 The Fresnel Equations

The previous two subsections focused on *where* light travels following an interaction with a specular material. We now turn to the question of *how much*?

> 前两小节关注的是光与镜面材料交互后*向何处*传播。现在我们转向*多少*的问题。

Light is normally both reflected and transmitted at the boundary between two materials with a different index of refraction, though the transmission rapidly decays in the case of conductors. For physically accurate rendering, we must account for the fraction of reflected and transmitted light, which is directionally dependent and therefore cannot be captured by a fixed per-surface scaling constant. The *Fresnel equations*, which are the solution to Maxwell's equations at smooth surfaces, specify the precise proportion of reflected light.

> 光在两种不同折射率材料的边界处通常同时被反射和透射，尽管对于导体透射会迅速衰减。为了物理精确的渲染，我们必须考虑反射和透射光的比例，该比例依赖于方向，因此不能用固定的逐表面缩放常数来捕获。*菲涅尔方程*是麦克斯韦方程在光滑表面处的解，指定了反射光的精确比例。

Recall the conscious decision to ignore polarization effects in Section [4.1](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Radiometry,_Spectra,_and_Color/Radiometry.html#sec:radiometry). In spite of that, we must briefly expand on how polarization is represented to express the Fresnel equations in their natural form that emerges within the framework of electromagnetism.

> 回想第 4.1 节中有意忽略偏振效应的决定。尽管如此，我们必须简要扩展偏振的表示方式，以便在电磁学框架中以其自然形式表达菲涅尔方程。

### 9.3.6 The Fresnel Equations for Conductors

Characterizing the reflection behavior of conductors involves an additional twist: the IOR turns into a complex number! Its real component describes the decrease in the speed of light as before. The newly added imaginary component models the decay of light as it travels deeper into the material. This decay occurs so rapidly that it also has a significant effect on the reflection component; hence it is important that we account for it even if the transmitted portion of light is of no interest.

> 表征导体的反射行为涉及一个额外的转折：折射率变成了一个复数！其实部如前所述描述光速的降低。新添加的虚部模拟了光在深入材料时的衰减。这种衰减发生得如此迅速，以至于它对反射分量也有显著影响；因此即使我们对透射光部分不感兴趣，也必须考虑它。
