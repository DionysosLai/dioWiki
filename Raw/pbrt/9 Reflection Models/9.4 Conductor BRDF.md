## 9.4 Conductor BRDF

Having described the relevant physical principles, we now turn to the implementation of a BRDF that models specular reflection from an interface between a dielectric (e.g., air or water) and a conductor (e.g., a polished metal surface). We initially focus on the smooth case, and later generalize the implementation to rough interfaces in Section [9.6](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Roughness_Using_Microfacet_Theory.html#sec:microfacet).

> 在描述了相关物理原理之后，我们现在转向实现一个 BRDF，该 BRDF 模拟从电介质（如空气或水）与导体（如抛光金属表面）之间界面的镜面反射。我们最初关注光滑情况，稍后在第 9.6 节将实现推广到粗糙界面。

The internal state of the ConductorBxDF consists of the real (eta) and imaginary (k) component of the index of refraction. Furthermore, the implementation requires a microfacet distribution that statistically describes its roughness. The [TrowbridgeReitzDistribution](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Roughness_Using_Microfacet_Theory.html#TrowbridgeReitzDistribution) class handles the details here. The constructor, not included here, takes these fields as input and stores them in the ConductorBxDF instance.

> ConductorBxDF 的内部状态由折射率的实部（eta）和虚部（k）组成。此外，该实现需要一个统计描述其粗糙度的微面元分布。TrowbridgeReitzDistribution 类在此处理细节。构造函数（此处未包含）将这些字段作为输入并存储在 ConductorBxDF 实例中。

The conductor BRDF builds on two physical ideas: the law of specular reflection assigns a specific reflected direction to each ray, and the Fresnel equations determine the portion of reflected light. Any remaining light refracts into the conductor, where it is rapidly absorbed and converted into heat.

> 导体 BRDF 建立在两个物理概念之上：镜面反射定律为每条光线分配一个特定的反射方向，菲涅尔方程确定反射光的比例。剩余的光折射进入导体，在那里被迅速吸收并转化为热能。

Note that Dirac delta distributions require special handling compared to standard functions. In particular, the probability of successfully drawing a point on the peak is zero, unless the sampling probability is also a delta distribution. In other words, the distribution must be used to determine the sample location.

> 请注意，与标准函数相比，狄拉克 δ 分布需要特殊处理。特别是，成功抽取到峰值点的概率为零，除非采样概率也是 δ 分布。换句话说，必须使用该分布来确定样本位置。

Because the surface normal is in the reflection coordinate system, the equation for the perfect specular reflection direction, ([9.1](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Specular_Reflection_and_Transmission.html#eq:specular-reflection-direction)), simplifies substantially; the and components only need to be negated to compute this direction and there is no need to call [Reflect()](https://pbr-book.org/4ed/Reflection_Models/Specular_Reflection_and_Transmission.html#Reflect) (the rough case will require this function, however).
