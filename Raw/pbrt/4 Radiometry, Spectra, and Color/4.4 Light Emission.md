## 4.4 Light Emission

The atoms of an object with temperature above absolute zero are moving. In turn, as described by Maxwell's equations, the motion of atomic particles that hold electrical charges causes objects to emit electromagnetic radiation over a range of wavelengths. As we will see shortly, at room temperature most of the emission is at infrared frequencies; objects need to be much warmer to emit meaningful amounts of electromagnetic radiation at visible frequencies.

> 温度高于绝对零度的物体的原子在运动。反过来，正如麦克斯韦方程组所描述的，带电原子粒子的运动导致物体在一系列波长上发射电磁辐射。我们很快会看到，在室温下大部分辐射在红外频率；物体需要更热得多才能在可见频率上发射有意义数量的电磁辐射。

Many different types of light sources have been invented to convert energy into emitted electromagnetic radiation. A number of corresponding types of lamps are in wide use today:

> 已经发明了许多不同类型的光源来将能量转换为发射的电磁辐射。目前广泛使用的几种对应类型的灯具包括：

- Incandescent (tungsten) lamps have a small tungsten filament. The flow of electricity through the filament heats it, which in turn causes it to emit electromagnetic radiation with a distribution of wavelengths that depends on the filament's temperature.

> - 白炽（钨丝）灯有一根细小的钨丝。电流通过灯丝加热它，进而使其发射电磁辐射，其波长分布取决于灯丝的温度。

- Halogen lamps also have a tungsten filament, but the enclosure around them is filled with halogen gas. The halogen gas causes evaporated tungsten to return to the filament, which lengthens the life of the light.

> - 卤素灯也有钨丝，但其周围的外壳充满卤素气体。卤素气体使蒸发的钨返回灯丝，从而延长了灯的寿命。

- Gas-discharge lamps pass electrical current through hydrogen, neon, argon, or vaporized metal gas, which causes light to be emitted at specific wavelengths that depend on the particular atom in the gas.

> - 气体放电灯使电流通过氢气、氖气、氩气或气化金属气体，这导致在取决于气体中特定原子的特定波长处发射光。

- LED lights are based on electroluminescence: they use materials that emit photons due to electrical current passing through them.

> - LED 灯基于电致发光：它们使用因电流通过而发射光子的材料。

*Luminous efficacy* measures how effectively a light source converts power to visible illumination, accounting for the fact that for human observers, emission in non-visible wavelengths is of little value. It has units of lumens per watt. A typical value for an incandescent tungsten lightbulb is around 15 lm/W. The highest value it can possibly have is 683, for a perfectly efficient light source that emits all of its light at 555 nm, the peak of the V(λ) function.

> *光效*衡量光源将功率转换为可见照明的有效程度，考虑到对人类观察者来说，非可见波长的辐射价值很小。其单位是流明/瓦特。白炽钨丝灯泡的典型值约为 15 lm/W。它可能达到的最高值是 683，适用于在 555 nm（V(λ) 函数的峰值）处发射全部光的完美高效光源。

### 4.4.1 Blackbody Emitters

A *blackbody* is a perfect emitter: it converts power to electromagnetic radiation as efficiently as physically possible. While true blackbodies are not physically realizable, some emitters exhibit near-blackbody behavior. Blackbodies are so-named because they absorb absolutely all incident power, reflecting none of it.

> *黑体*是完美的发射体：它将功率转换为电磁辐射的效率达到物理上可能的极限。虽然真正的黑体在物理上无法实现，但一些发射体表现出接近黑体的行为。黑体之所以如此命名，是因为它们绝对吸收所有入射功率，不反射任何。

*Planck's law* gives the radiance emitted by a blackbody as a function of wavelength λ and temperature T measured in kelvins (Equation 4.17). Blackbody emitters are perfectly diffuse; they emit radiance equally in all directions.

> *普朗克定律*给出黑体发射的辐射亮度作为波长 λ 和开尔文温度 T 的函数（方程 4.17）。黑体发射体是完全漫射的；它们在所有方向上均匀发射辐射亮度。

Figure 4.12: Plots of emitted radiance as a function of wavelength for blackbody emitters at a few temperatures. Note that as temperature increases, more of the emitted light is in the visible frequencies (roughly 380 nm–780 nm) and that the spectral distribution shifts from reddish colors to bluish colors.

> 图 4.12：几个温度下黑体发射体发射辐射亮度随波长变化的曲线。注意随着温度升高，更多的发射光在可见频率范围内（大约 380 nm–780 nm），且光谱分布从红色调向蓝色调转移。

The emission of non-blackbodies is described by *Kirchhoff's law*, which says that the emitted radiance distribution at any frequency is equal to the emission of a perfect blackbody at that frequency times the fraction of incident radiance at that frequency that is absorbed by the object (Equation 4.18).

> 非黑体的发射由*基尔霍夫定律*描述，该定律表明任何频率处发射的辐射亮度分布等于该频率处完美黑体的发射乘以该频率处被物体吸收的入射辐射亮度比例（方程 4.18）。

The *Stefan–Boltzmann law* gives the radiant exitance at a point for a blackbody emitter: M = σT⁴ (Equation 4.19). Note that the total emission over all frequencies grows very rapidly—at the rate T⁴. Thus, doubling the temperature of a blackbody emitter increases the total energy emitted by a factor of 16.

> *斯特藩-玻尔兹曼定律*给出黑体发射体某点处的辐射出射度：M = σT⁴（方程 4.19）。注意总发射在所有频率上增长非常迅速——以 T⁴ 的速率。因此，将黑体发射体的温度加倍会使总发射能量增加 16 倍。

The blackbody emission distribution provides a useful metric for describing the emission characteristics of non-blackbody emitters through the notion of *color temperature*. *Wien's displacement law* gives the wavelength where emission of a blackbody is maximum given its temperature: λ_max = b/T (Equation 4.20), where b is Wien's displacement constant.

> 黑体发射分布通过*色温*的概念为描述非黑体发射体的发射特性提供了有用的度量。*维恩位移定律*给出给定温度下黑体发射最大的波长：λ_max = b/T（方程 4.20），其中 b 是维恩位移常数。

Incandescent tungsten lamps are generally around 2700 K color temperature, and tungsten halogen lamps are around 3000 K. Fluorescent lights may range all the way from 2700 K to 6500 K. Generally speaking, color temperatures over 5000 K are described as "cool," while 2700–3000 K is described as "warm."

> 白炽钨丝灯通常约为 2700 K 色温，钨卤素灯约为 3000 K。荧光灯可能从 2700 K 到 6500 K 不等。一般来说，超过 5000 K 的色温被描述为"冷色"，而 2700–3000 K 被描述为"暖色"。

### 4.4.2 Standard Illuminants

Another useful way of categorizing light emission distributions is a number of "standard illuminants" that have been defined by Commission Internationale de l'Éclairage (CIE).

> 对光发射分布进行分类的另一种有用方式是国际照明委员会（CIE）定义的一系列"标准光源"。

The Standard Illuminant A was introduced in 1931 and was intended to represent average incandescent light. It corresponds to a blackbody radiator of about 2856 K.

> 标准光源 A 于 1931 年引入，旨在代表平均白炽光。它对应约 2856 K 的黑体辐射体。

Figure 4.13: Plot of the CIE Standard Illuminant A's Spectral Power Distribution as a Function of Wavelength in nm. This illuminant represents incandescent illumination.

> 图 4.13：CIE 标准光源 A 的光谱功率分布随波长（nm）变化的曲线。该光源代表白炽照明。

The D illuminant describes various phases of daylight. D65 is roughly 6504 K color temperature and is intended to correspond to mid-day sunlight in Europe. The CIE recommends that this illuminant be used for daylight unless there is a specific reason not to.

> D 光源描述日光的各个阶段。D65 大约为 6504 K 色温，旨在对应欧洲正午阳光。CIE 建议除非有特定原因否则使用此光源表示日光。

Figure 4.14: Plot of the CIE Standard D65 Illuminant Spectral Distribution as a Function of Wavelength in nm. This illuminant represents noontime daylight at European latitudes and is commonly used to define the whitepoint of color spaces.

> 图 4.14：CIE 标准 D65 光源光谱分布随波长（nm）变化的曲线。该光源代表欧洲纬度的正午日光，通常用于定义色彩空间的白点。

Finally, the F series of illuminants describes fluorescents; it is based on measurements of a number of actual fluorescent lights. Spikes in the distributions correspond to the wavelengths directly emitted by atoms in the gas, while the other wavelengths are generated by the bulb's fluorescent coating.

> 最后，F 系列光源描述荧光灯；它基于对多种实际荧光灯的测量。分布中的尖峰对应气体中原子直接发射的波长，而其他波长由灯泡的荧光涂层产生。

Figure 4.15: Plots of the F4 and F9 Standard Illuminants as a Function of Wavelength in nm. These represent two fluorescent lights. The F9 illuminant is a "broadband" emitter that uses multiple phosphors to achieve a more uniform spectral distribution.

> 图 4.15：F4 和 F9 标准光源随波长（nm）变化的曲线。它们代表两种荧光灯。F9 光源是一种"宽带"发射体，使用多种荧光粉实现更均匀的光谱分布。
