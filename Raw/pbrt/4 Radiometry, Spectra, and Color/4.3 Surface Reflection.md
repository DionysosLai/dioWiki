## 4.3 Surface Reflection

When light is incident on a surface, the surface scatters the light, reflecting some of it back into the environment. There are two main effects that need to be described to model this reflection: the spectral distribution of the reflected light and its directional distribution. For example, the skin of a lemon mostly absorbs light in the blue wavelengths but reflects most of the light in the red and green wavelengths. Therefore, when it is illuminated with white light, its color is yellow.

> 当光入射到表面时，表面散射光线，将其中一部分反射回环境。需要描述两个主要效应来模拟这种反射：反射光的光谱分布和方向分布。例如，柠檬的果皮大部分吸收蓝色波长的光，但反射大部分红色和绿色波长的光。因此，当它被白光照射时，颜色是黄色的。

Reflection from translucent surfaces is more complex; a variety of materials ranging from skin and leaves to wax and liquids exhibit *subsurface light transport*, where light that enters the surface at one point exits it some distance away. (Consider, for example, how shining a flashlight in one's mouth makes one's cheeks light up, as light that enters the inside of the cheeks passes through the skin and exits the face.)

> 半透明表面的反射更为复杂；从皮肤和树叶到蜡和液体的各种材料都表现出*次表面光传输*，即光在一个点进入表面后在一定距离外离开。（例如，想象一下在嘴里照手电筒如何使脸颊发光，因为进入脸颊内侧的光穿过皮肤从面部射出。）

There are two abstractions for describing these mechanisms for light reflection: the BRDF and the BSSRDF, described in Sections 4.3.1 and 4.3.2, respectively. The BRDF describes surface reflection at a point neglecting the effect of subsurface light transport. The BSSRDF generalizes the BRDF and describes the more general setting of light reflection from translucent materials.

> 有两种抽象用于描述这些光反射机制：BRDF 和 BSSRDF，分别在第 4.3.1 节和第 4.3.2 节中描述。BRDF 描述某点处的表面反射，忽略次表面光传输的效应。BSSRDF 推广了 BRDF，描述了半透明材料光反射的更一般情况。

### 4.3.1 The BRDF and the BTDF

The *bidirectional reflectance distribution function* (BRDF) gives a formalism for describing reflection from a surface. It defines the relationship between incident irradiance from direction ωᵢ and the differential reflected radiance in the viewing direction ωₒ.

> *双向反射分布函数*（BRDF）提供了描述表面反射的形式化方法。它定义了来自方向 ωᵢ 的入射辐照度与观察方向 ωₒ 的微分反射辐射亮度之间的关系。

Figure 4.10: The BRDF. The bidirectional reflectance distribution function is a 4D function over pairs of directions ωᵢ and ωₒ that describes how much incident light along ωᵢ is scattered from the surface in the direction ωₒ.

> 图 4.10：BRDF。双向反射分布函数是关于方向对 ωᵢ 和 ωₒ 的 4D 函数，描述了沿 ωᵢ 入射的光中有多少从表面向方向 ωₒ 散射。

The constant of proportionality between differential irradiance and differential reflected radiance defines the surface's BRDF, f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ) = dLₒ(p, ωₒ) / dE(p, ωᵢ) (Equation 4.11).

> 微分辐照度与微分反射辐射亮度之间的比例常数定义了表面的 BRDF，f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ) = dLₒ(p, ωₒ) / dE(p, ωᵢ)（方程 4.11）。

Physically based BRDFs have two important qualities:

> 基于物理的 BRDF 具有两个重要性质：

1. *Reciprocity:* For all pairs of directions ωᵢ and ωₒ, f(p, ωᵢ, ωₒ) = f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ).

> 1. *互易性：*对于所有方向对 ωᵢ 和 ωₒ，f(p, ωᵢ, ωₒ) = f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ)。

2. *Energy conservation:* The total energy of light reflected is less than or equal to the energy of incident light.

> 2. *能量守恒：*反射光的总能量小于或等于入射光的能量。

Note that the value of the BRDF for a pair of directions is *not* necessarily less than 1; it is only its integral that has this normalization constraint.

> 注意，一对方向的 BRDF 值*不一定*小于 1；只有其积分才有这个归一化约束。

Two quantities based on the BRDF will occasionally be useful. The *hemispherical-directional reflectance* ρ_hd gives the total reflection in a given direction due to constant illumination over the hemisphere (Equation 4.12). The *hemispherical-hemispherical reflectance* ρ_hh gives the fraction of incident light reflected by a surface when the incident light is the same from all directions (Equation 4.13).

> 基于 BRDF 的两个量偶尔会有用。*半球-方向反射率* ρ_hd 给出由半球上恒定照明引起的给定方向上的总反射（方程 4.12）。*半球-半球反射率* ρ_hh 给出当入射光从所有方向相同时表面反射的入射光比例（方程 4.13）。

A surface's *bidirectional transmittance distribution function* (BTDF), which describes the distribution of transmitted light, can be defined in a manner similar to that for the BRDF. Remarkably, the BTDF does not obey reciprocity as defined above; we will discuss this issue in detail in Section 9.5.2.

> 表面的*双向透射分布函数*（BTDF）描述了透射光的分布，可以用与 BRDF 类似的方式定义。值得注意的是，BTDF 不遵守上面定义的互易性；我们将在第 9.5.2 节中详细讨论这个问题。

For convenience in equations, we will denote the BRDF and BTDF when considered together as f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ); we will call this the *bidirectional scattering distribution function* (BSDF). Chapter 9 is entirely devoted to describing a variety of BSDFs that are useful for rendering.

> 为了方程的便利，我们将 BRDF 和 BTDF 一起考虑时用 f(p, ωₒ, ωᵢ) 表示；我们将其称为*双向散射分布函数*（BSDF）。第 9 章完全致力于描述对渲染有用的各种 BSDF。

We can integrate the scattering equation over the sphere of incident directions to compute the outgoing radiance in direction ωₒ due to the incident illumination from all directions (Equation 4.14). This is a fundamental equation in rendering; it describes how an incident distribution of light at a point is transformed into an outgoing distribution, based on the scattering properties of the surface. It is often called the *scattering equation* when the sphere is the domain, or the *reflection equation* when just the upper hemisphere is being integrated over.

> 我们可以在入射方向的球面上积分散射方程，以计算由所有方向入射照明引起的方向 ωₒ 的出射辐射亮度（方程 4.14）。这是渲染中的一个基本方程；它描述了基于表面的散射属性，某点处入射光分布如何转化为出射分布。当球面是积分域时通常称为*散射方程*，当仅在上半球上积分时称为*反射方程*。

### 4.3.2 The BSSRDF

The *bidirectional scattering surface reflectance distribution function* (BSSRDF) is the formalism that describes scattering from materials that exhibit subsurface light transport. It describes the ratio of exitant differential radiance at point pₒ in direction ωₒ to the incident differential flux at pᵢ from direction ωᵢ (Equation 4.15, Figure 4.11).

> *双向散射表面反射分布函数*（BSSRDF）是描述表现出次表面光传输的材料散射的形式化方法。它描述了点 pₒ 处方向 ωₒ 的出射微分辐射亮度与点 pᵢ 处来自方向 ωᵢ 的入射微分通量之比（方程 4.15，图 4.11）。

Figure 4.11: The bidirectional scattering surface reflectance distribution function generalizes the BSDF to account for light that exits the surface at a point other than where it enters. It is often more difficult to evaluate than the BSDF, although subsurface light transport makes a substantial contribution to the appearance of many real-world objects.

> 图 4.11：双向散射表面反射分布函数将 BSDF 推广为考虑光在不同于进入点处离开表面的情况。虽然它通常比 BSDF 更难求值，但次表面光传输对许多真实物体的外观有重大贡献。

The generalization of the scattering equation for the BSSRDF requires integration over surface area *and* incoming direction, turning the 2D scattering Equation (4.14) into a 4D integral (Equation 4.16). With two more dimensions to integrate over, it is more complex to account for in rendering algorithms. However, as the distance between points pᵢ and pₒ increases, the value of S generally diminishes, which can be a substantial help in implementations.

> BSSRDF 的散射方程推广需要在表面积*和*入射方向上积分，将二维散射方程 (4.14) 转化为四维积分（方程 4.16）。需要多积分两个维度，这在渲染算法中更为复杂。然而，随着点 pᵢ 和 pₒ 之间距离的增加，S 的值通常会减小，这在实现中可以提供很大帮助。

Light transport beneath a surface is described by the same principles as volume light transport in participating media and is described by the equation of transfer, which is introduced in Section 14.1. Subsurface scattering is thus based on the same effects as light scattering in clouds and smoke—just at a smaller scale.

> 表面下方的光传输与参与介质中的体积光传输遵循相同的原理，由传输方程描述，该方程在第 14.1 节中介绍。因此，次表面散射与云和烟中的光散射基于相同的效应——只是尺度更小。
